So, should you make any changes due to the protests?

In short, no.

News headlines can make it seem like the whole country is in an uproar, and tourists are being harassed left, right, and center.

Not at all.

The planned protests have been peaceful and organized. Theyâ€™ve been coordinated with local police and have happened at specific times and in specific places.

The women shooting the water pistols in Barcelona was a one-off event.

Of course, I don't want you to think I'm smoke-screening and telling you it's all hunky dory here.

This is indeed a summer where Spanish frustration with the mismanagement of mass tourism has hit a spike.

And, of course, if the idea of the protests and anti-tourist signs or graffiti makes you feel like spending your tourist euros elsewhere, thatâ€™s a perfectly reasonable decision to make.

But, it's important to say that most thoughtful Spaniards understand the value tourism brings to the local economy and are more concerned with how the authorities are managing growing tourism numbers, specifically when it comes to tourism accommodation and housing.

Most locals don't want to throw the tourism baby out with the bathwater.

Of course, there will always be a vocal minority who have not thought about the issues sufficiently and who misplace their frustration, such as with graffiti or signs against the tourists themselves.

And they get a lot of headlines.

But if you're concerned about safety or your plans being disrupted, then I certainly don't think you should change any plans for those reasons.

99% of tourists to Spain are having a great vacation and not encountering any issues or hostility.
