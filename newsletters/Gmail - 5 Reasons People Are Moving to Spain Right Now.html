5 Reasons People Are Moving to Spain Right Now

I remember the moment I knew I had to live in Spain.

When I first arrived in Europe, it was like stepping into an electric current.

The history, the energy, the sense of movement—it felt alive in a way I hadn’t experienced before. And I knew I wanted to be part of it.

But I was also moving away from something.

New Zealand is an incredible place, but for me, it always felt too small, too far away. I wanted to be in the middle of things, not on the periphery.

And that’s what I’ve come to realize—moving abroad is never just about what you’re leaving behind, and it’s never just about what you’re moving toward.

It’s always a blend of both.

And depending on the person, depending on the timing, that balance shifts.

Sometimes the pull of a new life is stronger than the push away from the old one.

Other times, the urgency to leave outweighs everything else.

I see this duality again and again in the stories of people looking to start a new chapter here.

People come in at different points in their lives, with different motivations, but there are always common themes.

So I want to share with you the five biggest reasons why I see people moving to Spain right now.

Some of these may resonate with you, depending on where you’re at in your life, and show you that you’re not alone in thinking about this change.

1. Lifestyle

Many people are exhausted.

Hustle culture, long work hours, and the constant pressure to be productive. Spain offers an alternative—a place where life isn’t structured around constant busyness.

That doesn’t necessarily mean long tapas lunches every day or any other slightly overworked Spanish lifestyle cliché.

Those things exist, sure, but the reality runs deeper.

It’s about the rhythm of life here.

And the culture's priorities.

The pace isn’t dictated by an obsession with efficiency.

There’s space to breathe, to connect, to be present.

(But old habits die hard—if you’re used to always being ‘on,’ you might have to work at rewiring your internal programming a little once you arrive in Spain.)

You don’t feel constantly pushed to keep up—because the culture around you isn’t demanding that you do.

In a sense, Spain provides people with the opportunity for a lifestyle that inherently we know is healthier.

And try as they might, it's just really hard to access that lifestyle back home.

And in Spain, there is the chance to achieve it.

2. Climate

Sun and sand have long been the archetypal draw for many people holidaying in Spain.

And while Spain has its own climate challenges, the simple reality of living somewhere with abundant sunshine remains a powerful draw.

But I see more and more people realizing that Spain offers incredible variety when it comes to climate.

There are regions with hot, dry summers and others with cool, misty winters.

Some places get snow. Others are mild year-round.

From the crisp mountain air of the Pyrenees to the lush greenery of Galicia to the steady sunshine of Andalusia, Spain has a landscape and climate to suit almost any preference.

In fact, I often see couples where one person dreams of the warm, dry south while the other is drawn to the fresher, greener north.

And that’s a conversation they have to navigate—through online research, scouting trips, and plenty of deep discussions—until they find the place that truly feels like home for both of them.

3. Cost of Living

Let’s be clear—Spain isn’t "cheap."

And to talk about it as if it were can feel tone-deaf and dismissive of the financial struggles people face here.

But for many people moving from overseas, life in Spain does provide financial relief.

Rent, healthcare, and daily expenses can be significantly more affordable than back home.

And this is especially critical if you're retiring and will be living on a fixed income.

But beyond just the numbers, the way life is lived is different.

In some places, filling your time often means spending money—on entertainment, activities, or dining out.

But in Spain, the culture itself creates opportunities to enjoy life without a price tag.

Taking a walk through a historic town, lingering over a coffee, or chatting with the guy behind the bar aren’t just things to do in between obligations—they are the experience.

These moments matter—and they cost little, if anything at all.

4. Stage of Life

For many people, moving to Spain is about timing.

I see a lot of early retirees who are looking not just to stop working sooner, but to retire with less anxiety about the future.

After decades of work and saving, they want to enjoy their time without the financial and healthcare worries that might weigh on them elsewhere.

But I also see younger people and families looking for something different—something broader than just an escape from work.

Many want a cultural experience, the chance to raise their children bilingual, or simply to live in a place where work-life balance feels more achievable.

It’s about choosing a different kind of life at a pivotal moment.

And for a growing number of people, that choice is becoming more urgent.

5. Politics & Safety

Especially among Americans, I’ve seen a shift.

It’s not just dissatisfaction with the political climate in the US—it’s disenchantment at best, and outright fear at worst.

For some, depending on their beliefs or simply who they are, there’s a growing sense that they no longer feel safe or secure where they are.

Spain isn’t perfect, and it has its own political complexities.

But for many, it still feels like a place where the culture isn’t stuck in a constant state of Defcon 1—where every conversation, every decision, doesn’t feel like a battle in a larger ideological war.

In short, it’s a place where people can actually live—where they don’t have to brace for conflict, or worry that who they are or what they believe makes them a target.

A place where they can build a life that feels free, full, and theirs.

For many, Spain was always in the back of their mind—something to consider one day.

But this is what’s turning 'one day' into 'right now'. The urgency is real, and more people are deciding they can’t wait any longer.

If this has been on your mind, if you’re starting to feel that quiet (or not-so-quiet) pull toward something different, then you're not alone.
