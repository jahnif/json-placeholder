Elegant Night Trains are back in Spain & Europe

Like many of you, I love traveling by fast train in Spain. The experience is so much more relaxing than flying.

But Spain and Europe's fast train options are only just kicking off.

Soon, there will be plenty of options to travel across Europe by night aboard high-speed trains.

Starting in December 2024, the Austrian initiative Nightjet will launch a luxurious overnight train service connecting Barcelona with Zurich. â€‹

This marks the beginning of a broader plan to link Barcelona with key European cities like Basel, Berlin, Brussels, Cologne, Florence, Hamburg, and Milan.

Some Nightjet routes are already in operation.

Seats start at â‚¬50, with private sleeper cars costing about â‚¬160. Cabins boast a built-in bathroom, and porters serve an a la carte breakfast in the sleeper cars.

What's more, by booking an overnight train, you save on paying a night in a hotel.

Once a symbol of luxury and adventure, overnight train travel was a common and often glamorous way to cross Europe. However, in recent years, night trains have gone out of vogue.

Happily, we're witnessing a resurgence of long-distance overnight train travel, hopefully bringing a little romance and charm back to European travel. Short-haul airlines are incredibly handy, but the experience leaves much to be desired.

The push for night trains is also part of a European plan to promote train travel as a greener alternative to air travel. Countries like France are leading the way, banning domestic flights for distances under 200 kilometers. Spain is also considering such a rule. The European Union is also encouraging member states to enhance train networks.

Another company, Midnight Trains, also plans to start operations by the end of 2024, offering overnight connections between Paris and Barcelona, among other cities.
