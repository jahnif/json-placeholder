Spain's first openly LGBT matador says more will follow

Mario Alcalde, Spain's first openly LGBT matador, has stirred both the bullfighting and LGBT+ communities by revealing his pansexuality.

Working full-time as a baggage handler at Madrid airport, Alcalde's coming out is unexpected in a tradition that is famously associated with conservative Spanish values.

Upcoming Immigration Reform Will (Hopefully!) Simplify Bureaucracy

Spain's immigration minister has announced that in the first quarter of 2024, they're going to overhaul the country's immigration processes with the aim of simplifying them.

One of the biggest challenges of moving to Spain is dealing with the often arcane and confusing immigration law. And this opacity isn't helped by the sense that Spain's immigration departments are difficult to deal with.

So, a key aim of the upcoming reform is to streamline the country's immigration process. The reform aims to simplify things by reducing and clarifying the number of residency permit categories, making it easier for prospective immigrants to understand and navigate the system.

Plus, the minister announced a shift in perspective within Spanish immigration offices. The idea will be to encourage a more welcoming, inclusive approach and move away from the historically restrictive interpretation of immigration laws.

While details are still scarce, hopefully, the reform will make one of the most daunting aspects of moving to Spain a little more approachable.

But one thing is clear: if you're applying for some of Spain's most common visas (Digital Nomad, Non-Lucrative, Golden Visa, etc.), those won't be altered by this reform (which is all about streamlining).
