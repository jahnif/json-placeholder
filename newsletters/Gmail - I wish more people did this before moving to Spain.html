She thought she had it all figured out.

Spain—sun, tapas, adventure. A fresh start.

But a year after moving, she was back in the U.S., calling it quits. The reality of life in Spain wasn’t what she expected.

She had the dream… but not the vision.

I hate to flog a dead horse. But here I am, talking again about the couple in the CNN article whose move-to-Spain dream became a disaster.

See, I just think it's a valuable case study in the basic mistakes people make when they don’t take the time to get specific about their future life.

Last week, I touched on the five biggest mistakes (you can read that here).

But this week, I want to talk about something even more foundational...

Vision.

Here’s what I see all the time…

People move to Spain thinking they know what life will be like.

Lazy lunches… spontaneous weekend getaways… a picture-perfect Mediterranean lifestyle.

But then reality kicks in.

  • Life moves at its own rhythm. Bureaucracy, business hours, and social schedules don’t always align with what you’re used to.
  • The language barrier is real. Even basic tasks can feel overwhelming.
  • The social dynamic takes effort. Making friends isn’t automatic.
  • The cost of living can surprise you—some things are cheaper, others aren’t.
  • It’s not just about sunshine. Humidity, wind, and temperature extremes play a huge role in daily comfort, depending on where you settle.

You might be used to seeing me gallivanting around tapas bars.

But here's me, trying desperately to get my NZ driver's license converted to a Spanish one.

(Don't ask me about that process.)

In short, here's me living a real life in Spain.

Now, don't get me wrong.

Life in Spain should be amazing.

And if you do your prep, it WILL be amazing.

But in order to actually live a light, joyful life here, you need to put in a bit of work upfront.

(And don’t worry, it’s actually fun.)

Because if you don’t have a clear, detailed vision of what your day-to-day life will look like…

You risk feeling frustrated. Disappointed. Even stuck.

The solution? Get ruthlessly clear on your vision.

Consider hyper-specific questions like—

  • Who will you actually be hanging out with? Fellow foreigners? Locals? A mix? (How’s your Spanish?). Or perhaps this will evolve over time?
  • What are your leisure non-negotiables? Museums? Cafés? Beaches? Hiking? (Because every place has a different mix of these…)
  • What’s your weather dealbreaker? Too much rain? Too much heat? Strong winds? (Because Spain has all of it, depending on where you are.)
  • Does your property need an elevator? Or are you comfortable walking up a flight of stairs with heavy shopping from the market? (Yeah, this is niche... but important!)
  • Will the kids play a sport or take specific extracurricular activities and need a pre-built community for that?
  • How close does the nearest hospital need to be? Do you require specific medical care, or is a general practitioner enough?
  • When you're traveling, in Spain or Europe, what kind of traveler are you — camping, modest hotels or luxury? Or a mix? This will impact your budget.

(This is just a random selection from the 70+ very drilled-down questions in my Vision Checklist that participants fill out at the start of my Move to Spain Masterclass.)

You see, it’s not about killing the dream through over-planning.

It's just that the devil really is in the details.

If you don’t get specific, the Spain you choose might not be the Spain you imagined.

And let’s be honest…

It won’t always be easy.

There will be bumps—frustrations, cultural surprises, days when you question everything.

But when your vision is clear, those bumps are just speed humps… not brick walls (or one-way plane tickets home...)

So in the lead-up to your move, sit down—wine optional—and close your eyes. Really picture what the nitty-gritty of life is going to look like.

Zoom in past the hazy, rose-tinted glasses of tapas feasts, endless summers, and European jaunts.

What does daily life actually look like? The routines, the small moments, the reality.

Because you're not only planning a move to Spain. You're planning your future life.

And the clearer your vision, the better that life will be.

Oh, and pro tip: If you’re moving with someone, do this separately… then compare notes. 😅
