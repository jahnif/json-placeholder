7 Mistakes to Avoid when Moving to Spain

Here's a quick roundup of the most common mistakes people make when moving to Spain.

1. Misunderstanding the Role of Moving Professionals

Mistake: Thinking one person can manage your entire move or not knowing who you need for each step.

Solution:

  • Understand how the puzzle of professionals fits together. In other words, what does an immigration lawyer or consultant do, how can a tax lawyer help, when do you need a relocation agent and when you need a buyerâ€™s agent?
  • Assuming youâ€™re not from the EU, generally, the first person you need to speak to is an immigration lawyer or consultant (to understand your legal path to life in Spain).
  • Tax and immigration are two different issues, but they go hand in hand and involve different professionals. Once you know your legal path or possible paths, you need a tax lawyer to understand the tax implications of that path or those paths.
  • Relocation agents help you once you're in country (renting, schools, paperwork etc.)
  • If youâ€™re looking to buy a house, you need a buyerâ€™s agent to help you find your dream home and advocate for your rights.
  • If you have already identified the place you want to purchase, by all means, find a lawyer who can guide you in the purchase process.

Often, professionals and firms offer more than one service. I know great tax lawyers who also do conveyancing.

But even though the professional youâ€™re working with offers more than one service, you donâ€™t have to use them for everything. Never feel railroaded into using a professional.

2. Choosing the Wrong Experts

Mistake: Hiring professionals who may not have the right experience for your specific situation.

  • Once you have a list of names, check their online reviews to build a picture of their service (check Google Reviews, Facebook reviews on their Facebook page, and Trustpilot)
  • Arrange an initial consultation to gauge their expertise with cases like yours and to assess their communication style. The critical question is this: Do they instill you with trust? If it's a no, then move on.

3. Navigating Visa and Immigration Incorrectly

Mistake: Not understanding which visa option is best for your situation.

  • If you decide to get the help of an immigration lawyer or consultant, first tell them your plan for life in Spain and then ask them what visas will enable you to live that life.

5. Overlooking Tax Implications
