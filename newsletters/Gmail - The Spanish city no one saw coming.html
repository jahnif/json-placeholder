The Spanish City No One Saw Coming

I’ll be honest.

If you had asked me 5+ years ago about the Basque city of Bilbao, I would have said it was a grey, industrial northern city.

Yep, I’m an idiot.

But I’m not the only one.

Compared to people's perception of sexy, fun San Sebastian, many think Bilbao is just, well, a big, grey, industrial city.

But the perception is changing.

Bilbao is growing in popularity not just as a tourist destination but also as a place to live.

Why?

First, there’s a growing trend of people favoring northern Spain and seeking cooler climates and alternatives to traditional Spanish sun-and-sand destinations.

But specifically, Bilbao, which has spent the last 20+ years reinventing itself, is finally getting its moment in the sun (perhaps not literally, but at least figuratively 😜).

In the 19th and 20th centuries, Bilbao was an industrial powerhouse renowned for shipbuilding and ironworks.

The estuary's banks bustled with factories and docks.

In recent years, though, the city has transformed into a more walkable, modern space.

Old industrial areas have given way to parks, smart urban planning, and iconic landmarks like the Guggenheim Museum.

And yep, it also has an epic food scene, just like San Sebastian.

In the last year or so, I’ve noticed more people in my Move to Spain Masterclass mention it as a possible place to relocate to.

And I think as smaller San Sebastian (which I love, by the way) deals with housing shortages, pricey apartments, and an increasingly touristy old town, Bilbao is becoming more popular.

And this brings me to Getxo, the leafy coastal town about 15 minutes from Bilbao.

During Bilbao’s industrial boom, Getxo was home to wealthy shipbuilders and industrialists, boasting some seriously impressive mansions (scroll back up to the second-to-last photo above).

Today, Getxo functions as a seaside suburb of Bilbao, with many residents commuting to work in the city.

And it’s a lovely spot—right on the water, with long beaches and a more relaxed vibe than Bilbao, yet only 15 minutes away by car.

So, if you're planning a trip to northern Spain or considering moving there, don't forget Getxo.

(And be sure to hit the bustling pintxo bars in the Puerto Viejo – Old Port – on the weekend).

The Secret Weapon for a Smooth Move to Spain: Relocation Consultants

While in Getxo, I had a chance to grab coffee with Natalia, a local relocation consultant.

Born and raised in the area, Natalia helps people who are moving to the Basque Country set up their new lives.

During our chat, we reflected on how the role of a relocation consultant is often misunderstood in Spain.

Let me explain what it is and why it’s one of the most underrated professionals when you’re moving to Spain.

The term “relocation consultant” or “relocation agent” might suggest someone who handles your entire move.

However, their role is more focused: they help you set up your life once you’re in the country (although you get in touch with them some months before your move).

In short, they assist you with:

  • Finding and securing a rental
  • Managing necessary bureaucratic paperwork upon arrival (e.g., obtaining your padrón or TIE card)
  • Opening a bank account
  • Setting up utilities and internet
  • Finding the right school for your kids
  • Any other essentials to start your life in Spain

I really believe working with a relocation consultant can be a wise investment.

Arriving in a new country can be stressful, but having a local contact to help you get set up can significantly smooth the process.

Typically, you can opt for a full package of services or select just a few, paying for what you need.

Prices vary widely. Previously, to help you find an apartment, they’d charge one month’s rent. These days, given the high demand for their services, it’s usually a bit more.

They’ve become increasingly crucial in popular cities like Valencia, Madrid, Barcelona, and Malaga, where securing a rental can be challenging.

The relocation consultant steps in to find a rental that meets your needs, pitch you to the owner (which is key in hot markets), negotiate terms, review the contract, and get you settled.

But here’s an important point: while some professionals who can help with your move to Spain operate nationally (e.g., immigration lawyers or tax attorneys), relocation agents (like real estate buyer’s agents) are ideally local to the area.

In a country where it’s all about who you know, you’re paying to leverage their local knowledge and contacts.

A classic example is opening a bank account. People often ask me which is the best brick-and-mortar bank in Spain (which I recommend you have in addition to online options like Wise).

The answer?

Sorry, there isn’t one.

(Let me back up a bit—Sabadell is a great choice. They’re really stepping up to serve foreign residents with their ‘Welcome Hub’ branches, making the process much smoother.)

But when it comes down to it, the best bank is the one where you have the ear of the branch manager.

And guess what? Your relocation agent will connect you with the branch manager they know, increasing your chances of getting concierge service.
