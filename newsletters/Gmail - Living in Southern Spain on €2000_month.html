Important visa updates

If you’re interested in moving to Spain or are in the process of doing so, it’s helpful to stay updated on how the Spanish authorities are handling the key visas and residencies.

So I asked Marina, co-founder of leading immigration firm Bureaucracy, about the visa trends she’s observed over the past few months.

Here’s what she said:

Digital Nomad Visa (DNV)

There haven’t been any significant changes to the Digital Nomad Visa application process in recent months.

However, here’s an important update for those applying as freelancers: the authorities are now putting a stronger emphasis on promptly registering as self-employed after you receive your residency.

If you don’t register as freelance within the first couple of months after your successful application, you might receive a notification from the UGE (the government department that handles DNV applications) revoking your visa—which could mean you’d have to leave the country immediately.

Signing up as a freelancer means registering with the social security department and the tax authorities.

This process can be a bit confusing for newbies, so I recommend getting a lawyer or gestor to help with it.

Non-Lucrative Visa (NLV)

The consulates are currently struggling with high workloads, particularly during the summer when many students are applying for visas.

This has led to significant delays in scheduling appointments, especially in Los Angeles and across all consulates in Canada.

We hope the situation will improve by September.

Golden Visa

There are no new updates at this time.

The visa remains in force until further notice, and our clients are still getting approved.
