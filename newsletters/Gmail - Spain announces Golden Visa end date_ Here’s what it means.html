Hi Joseph,

After months of speculation and whispers, we finally have concrete news: Spain's Golden Visa program will end on April 3, 2025.

Let me explain exactly what this means for you if you've been actively planning to apply or are just considering it.

What Happens After April 3, 2025?

First, here is some good news for current Golden Visa holders - you can relax.

Your status is secure, and the changes won't affect you.

Specifically:

  • You'll keep renewing your visa under the existing rules, just as you have been
  • After five years, you can transition to permanent residency, simplifying everything, from eliminating the need to renew your residency to freeing you from the property investment requirement.

Think of it like this: if you're already in, you're in.

The door isn't closing behind you.

Is There Still Time to Apply?

The short answer is yes - but your situation will determine how realistic this is.

Let me break down the three main scenarios I’m seeing:

1. I have already purchased a qualifying property (€500,000+):

You’re in the best position possible. If your property meets all the criteria, you’ve got a clear path forward:

  • There’s plenty of time to complete your application
  • You can focus on getting the application paperwork exactly right
  • Next Step: Contact a trusted immigration firm now to ensure your application is filed properly. Golden Visa applications can be tricky, so I don’t recommend applying on your own.

2. I have found a qualifying property but haven’t purchased:

You’re still in a good position, but timing becomes more critical:

  • Without financing, three months is typically enough to finalize the purchase and file your Golden Visa application
  • But you’ll need a coordinated team to make it happen smoothly
  • Next Step: Get an experienced lawyer to handle the purchase process, and have the immigration firm lined up to handle the Golden Visa application.

I checked with Marina, the founder of the immigration firm Bureaucracy, to see her perspective on people in this situation.

“It’s important to involve an immigration consultant early in the process—don’t wait until after the property purchase is complete. While your lawyer is handling the purchase, an immigration consultant can start key parts of your Golden Visa application, such as obtaining your criminal background check and preparing other necessary documents. These processes can overlap, saving you valuable time and ensuring your application is ready to go as soon as the purchase is finalized.”

3. I haven’t found a property yet:

This is where things get interesting.

It’s tight, but it could work with the right approach.

Today, I spoke with Agnes, an excellent Madrid-based buyer’s agent who shared this perspective:

Just last month, I worked with a client who went from our first Zoom call to signing property deeds within four weeks. Now, I want to be clear - this isn't typical. Everything had to align perfectly: the buyer knew exactly what they wanted, had all the documents and funds ready, and we found the right property immediately. A more normal timeline would be longer, but it shows what's possible with preparation and focus."

What Are Your Alternative Residency Options?

The end of the Golden Visa doesn’t mean the end of your Spanish dreams.

Let’s look at two popular alternatives that might actually work better for your situation:

Non-Lucrative Visa

The Non-Lucrative Visa is well-suited for retirees or anyone with savings and/or reliable passive income.

Key points

  • Requires significantly less financial investment than the Golden Visa - around €36,000 in savings or passive income for a couple rather than the €500,000 for the property investment
  • Offers more flexibility since you’re not tied to maintaining a specific property (and thus you can move to a different part of Spain if your first location doesn’t work out).
  • Unlike the Golden Visa, you’ll need to become a tax resident (spending more than 183 days yearly in Spain each year)/
  • Unlike the Golden Visa, you can’t work (and yes, that means ANY work, whether part-time, casual or remote).

​

Digital Nomad Visa

The Digital Nomad Visa is perfect if you work remotely for (predominantly) non-Spanish clients.

Key points:

  • Provides a relatively easy immigration route for those who want to live in Spain and work for clients back home.
  • Not currently available for US employees (you’d have to go freelance).
  • Has an income requirement (about €44,000 per year for a couple).
  • Unlike the Golden Visa, you'll need to become a tax resident (spending more than 183 days yearly in Spain each year).

A Gap in Coverage

Something I’ve noticed in my Move to Spain Masterclass is that many people who consider the Golden Visa are either retired or semi-retired but still want to keep a small sideline—whether that’s selling their artwork, running an Etsy shop, or taking on occasional freelance projects.

The Golden Visa has been perfect for this because it allows work without specifying income levels or the type of work permitted.

Unfortunately, the alternatives don’t fill this need.

The Digital Nomad Visa requires a minimum qualifying income and only applies to remote work for overseas clients, while the Non-Lucrative Visa prohibits any form of work altogether.

This leaves a real gap for those who want to live in Spain full-time but also maintain small, casual sources of income on the side.

Key Points to Remember

  • April 3, 2025: That’s your final deadline for new Golden Visa applications
  • Current holders: Nothing changes for you - your renewals continue as normal
  • Alternative visas exist, but they come with different requirements
