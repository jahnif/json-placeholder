For years, this was a source of tension between my Spanish wife Yoly and me.

Puentes—those long weekends that Spaniards seem to have coded into their DNA.

In Spanish, puente means “bridge.” It happens when a national holiday falls just before or after the weekend—say, a Thursday or a Monday.

People take the extra day off, often using some annual leave, and suddenly they’ve turned a standard weekend into a four—or even five—day escape.

Sometimes, if the holidays line up just right, people extend it further, almost taking a full week off.

They’re built into the calendar. Into the culture. Into the collective mindset.

It seems everyone here just knows when a puente is coming and plans accordingly.

Everyone… except me.

I never knew when they were. And that was a problem.

Yoly: “We’re going away this weekend, right?”

Me: “Wait… what? I have work.”

Yoly: *sighs* “James… it’s a puente.”

Again and again, I’d be scheduling work stuff, totally out of sync with the rhythm of life here.

Spaniards don’t need a reminder—these long weekends are second nature.

But for me, they weren’t.

And in a way, they still aren’t.

So, I came up with a system.

Every two months, my phone pings me: Check for upcoming puentes.

Yep, not sexy.

But very effective (ask Yoly!).

Even after 14 years, I’ve learned that integration doesn’t just happen—you have to work at it. Intentionally.

So last weekend, we took a trip.

It wasn't exactly a puente—but school kids had Friday and Monday off, so we headed up to Yoly's parents' place in Segovia for a long weekend.

The timing was perfect because Yoly’s parents had two tonnes of firewood delivered—and it needed stacking.

So there I was, piling up logs, all thanks to that (very unsexy) phone reminder.

And really, that’s what integration is—it’s not flashy, just small, deliberate steps that add up over time.

I guess it's like learning a language.

You don’t wake up one day fluent in Spanish just because you live here.

You have to study, practice, make mistakes (so many mistakes).

And slowly, it sticks.

It’s the same with settling into Spain.

It takes effort—getting in sync with the culture, embracing the quirks, and finding your own rhythm… even if it takes 14 years (and a few calendar reminders).

That’s why an entire module of my Move to Spain Masterclass, called Thrive, is dedicated to helping you do exactly that.

Because moving here is one thing, but thriving here? That’s a whole different (and ongoing) adventure.

Stacking wood in rural Segovia included...
