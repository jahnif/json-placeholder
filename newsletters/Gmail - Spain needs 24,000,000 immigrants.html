Spain needs 24,000,000 immigrants

That figure — 24,000,000 — is mind-blowing.

The country currently only has 48,000,000 inhabitants.

But here’s the thing: like many countries, Spain has an aging population.

Plus, it has a robust social welfare and pension system.

And both those things are on a collision course.

A recent report by the National Institute of Statistics has revealed that Spain will need three times the number of foreign residents by 2053 than is currently projected.

(You can translate the linked article by using Chrome, right clicking on the text, and selecting “Translate”)

That equates to 24,000,000 more working immigrants than the current number (currently 13,000,000) to maintain a sustainable balance between workers and pensioners.

In short, Spain needs immigrants to help maintain its social welfare system.

This is part of the reason for the recent Digital Nomad Visa.

This has brought in an influx of people, many of them freelancers, paying directly into the social welfare coffers.

And there is currently a bill going through the Spanish parliament that will regularize the status of about 500,000 undocumented immigrants.

What’s more, the bill will reduce barriers for those who want to work in Spain.

The Prime Minister, Pedro Sánchez, recently in Brussels, explained the law saying, “Legal immigration is a way of financing our economies,” referring to Europe as a whole.

But the elephant in the room is what Spaniards think about all of this.

Currently, Spaniards cite immigration as one of their key concerns (presumably fearing immigrants will take up local jobs and resources).

Meanwhile, employers are desperate for workers from abroad to fill vacancies that they can’t fill locally.

The key issue is that I don’t think many Spaniards realize how important immigrants are to the economy, and to the sustainability our much beloved social welfare system.

And the root cause?

I don’t think the government has explained this well.

The government is going to have to educate the population about why we now have a Digital Nomad Visa, or are regularizing undocumented immigrants.

They’re also going to need to build homes at a much faster clip than they currently are.

The 24,000,000 isn’t a goal that’s been set by the government.

It’s simply the reality as revealed by the study.

But while that goal might be unrealistic, it’s clear that in the coming years, Spain will continue to aggressively attract foreign workers to the country.

I just hope this and future governments are smart about laying the approapriate groundwork.
