This is the Spanish city where expats are the happiest

Meanwhile, I'll bet everyone was having a great time in Malaga, in southern Spain.

According to this article, it's where expats (or migrants) say they're the happiest.

Why? The locals are extremely friendly, with 89% of expats feeling warmly welcomed, way above the global average. Malaga's fantastic weather clearly plays a big part, as well as easy access to the sea and nature.

Plus, take it from me, the food is excellent.

But to give you more context of life on the ground, I interviewed our resident Andalusia expert and Malaga local Hanni Martini to see what life is like on the ground in Malaga.

What's life like in Spain's expat paradise?

Hanni Martini is a food, wine, and Andalusia expert who runs The Food Experience Malaga. She offers food & wine tours in Malaga capital. You'll also remember her from my most recent food tour video in Malaga.

And so I took the opportunity to ask her about life in Malaga...

💬 How long have you lived in Malaga? What brought you there?

I moved to Malaga in 2015. In 2011, I had a wonderful holiday in Estepona, and in just one week on the gorgeous Costa del Sol, I felt so at home. I just loved the way of life. Like most Brits, I got on the plane home and dreamed of my life in Spain. That was the beginning of the story, and long story short, four years later, I had sold everything in England and was on a ferry from Plymouth to Santander. For good!

💬 Which part of Malaga do you live in?

I live in Malaga City – the capital city of the province of Malaga.

💬 What are the positives of living in Malaga?

Where do I start? Malaga has it all. Located on the Med, with a backdrop of mountains, Malaga offers a diversity of landscapes like no other. The food is incredible, the people are so open, and the 3000-year history makes Malaga one of the most culturally diverse regions in the world. Malaga is exceptionally well-connected, with a major international airport and a fast train connection to Madrid and beyond. And, of course, let’s not overlook the climate - the best in Europe.

💬 What are the downsides (if any!)?

Honestly? I’m yet to find one!

💬 What do you do in your free time?

In Malaga, we’re very sociable. We spend a lot of time out and about socializing with family and friends. The weather permits year-round terrace dining. I’m a big food and wine enthusiast, so this is how I spend most of my time 😅

💬 What's one of your best memories of your time there?

I love La Fería de Malaga. It’s our summer fair, which takes place in August. It’s mad hot, and people dress up, drink Malaga wine, and dance flamenco in the streets. In the fairgrounds, people ride horses and carts and drink more Malaga wine. Some of my favorite memories are of fería with friends 💃🏼

💬 What three things should someone see/do/eat in Malaga?

Eat an espeto de sardinas. Fresh sardines from the Med skewered and cooked over a wood fire on the beach.

Visit the Roman Theatre, Alcazaba & Castillo del Gibralfaro. Two thousand years of history stacked one on top of the other.

Visit the Mercado Central de Atarazanas. This is special because at least 80% of the market produce is locally grown/produced. And you can buy and eat tapas and local wines in the market. The freshest tapas you can imagine.

💬 Anything else you want to add?

Malaga is generally little known by many travel agents and tour operators and, in my opinion, is underrated and, therefore, sadly missed by so many visitors to Spain. If you’re planning an itinerary that involves Andalusia, you should definitely include a day or two exploring everything that this beautiful city has to offer.

Thanks, Hanni!
