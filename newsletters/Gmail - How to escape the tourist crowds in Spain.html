How to avoid the tourist crush in Spain

1. Embrace shoulder seasons

Understandably, many people travel at the same time each year. But if you have any flexibility in your schedule, I suggest you give Spain a go in the "cooler" months.

First, here's the data: June through October are the busiest months for tourist arrivals, with September being the peak.

April & May follow, with slightly fewer tourists, but still busy.

Then, March and November (as well as the rest of the year) see a massive drop in tourist numbers.

In many parts of the country, the weather is excellent in March and early November, and it can be a great time to visit.

However, if you're heading north (Galicia, Asturias, etc), you might like to stick to the summer months or mid to late spring and early autumn, as it does get pretty cool and wet up there in the winter.

The benefits of visiting off-peak are apparent: lower hotel prices, shorter lines, fewer issues getting a table reservation, and more. I'd also argue you're going to find it easier not to feel like "just another tourist. "

Obviously, there is nothing wrong with being a tourist, but in prominent tourist destinations, restaurants and hotels go into survival mode just to keep up with numbers so that you might have a less personalized experience.

2. Get uncomfortable

In short, head off the well-worn tourist path.

Some of my best travel experiences have been when I've gotten out of the tourist center and stumbled into neighborhoods.

Sometimes, the experience can be a little bumpy (because these environments aren't as set up for tourists), but that makes the experience richer and more memorable (because it's less... standby for a power word... "curated").

And you don't have to head far to leave behind 90% of your fellow tourists.
