But then I discovered the bocadillos of Valencia. We featured a famous one in this video.

And now the secret is truly out, thanks to this article from the BBC.

The Valencian almuerzo (or esmorzaret in the Valencian language) is the food experience you never realised you were missing.

It kicks off with peanuts, olives, and a shot of cazalla (a local aniseed liqueur), and wraps up with a rum and lemon-infused coffee (cremaet).

But the heart of this meal is a serious bocadillo that can, literally, have anything in it.

Grilled sausage, gooey blood sausage, bacon, grilled peppers, cheese, egg, fries, broad beans, stewed pork, fresh tomato... I mean, anything.

And you can have any combination of any of the things.

As Flor of Casa Flor says in the article, there's no menu, but people tell her what ingredients they want in their sandwich, and she abides.

The tradition stems from the fact that workers in the market gardens around Valencia would start work early to avoid the hottest part of the day.

That meant by mid-morning, they were famished.

And as RaÃºl in our video explained (and based on how busy his restaurant Nuevo Oslo gets), the esmorzaret has become quite a popular local tradition in recent years. Groups of friends will get together, especially on the weekend, to eat large sandwiches, drink beer or wine and socialise.

In other words, it's the Valencian brunch.

So, if you're in the city, carve out a morning for a typical almuerzo at Casa Flor or Nuevo Oslo. You may have the best sandwich you've ever eaten in Spain.
