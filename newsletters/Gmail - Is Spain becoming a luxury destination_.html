Is Spain becoming a luxury destination?

Newsflash: 3 Spanish restaurants are in the top 5 of the World's 50 Best Restaurants.​

Another newsflash: Ronda is targeting luxury tourism, with 2 5-star hotels about to be built (link may require a subscription).

OK, and another newsflash: Magaluf on Mallorca, long known for bachelor parties and 2-for-1 shots, has also gone upmarket.​

And yep, Benidorm too.

I'm starting to see a trend, and I wonder...

Is Spain starting to angle more to the upscale end of the tourism market?

I wonder if a country long known as a cost-effective destination is tiring of that reputation.

More likely, tourism operators see that there is good money to be made in catering to those with fat(ter) wallets.

In many ways, it makes sense.

As the tourism pie grows (and it's growing in Spain, with more visitors each year), different tourism market segments, including the luxury segment, continue to grow.

And I think some destinations, like Magaluf, that once pursued tourism at any cost have now realized that party tourism is a hard road to hoe.

I've never been to Magaluf, but check out the photo below. It's beautiful. Anywhere else in the world, it'd be full of luxury resorts.

I get it. If I ran a restaurant in Magaluf, I'd prefer clients sipping wine than shooting shots.

But Spain has to be careful that we don't lose what attracts so many of us in the first place.

Most of us don't have a luxury budget, and when we travel to places like Spain, we don't want to shoot shots but we also don't want Michelin-star meals.

We want something in between: true, local experiences accompanied by Spain's famous down-home cooking and warm hospitality.

Let's hope that segment of the market remains alive and kicking.
