The Spanish city I'd move to next

I feel like I’ve been raving about Bilbao a lot lately.

I used to picture the city as gray and industrial, and indeed, it once was (you can read about Bilbao’s transformation here).

But after filming two videos there and spending a weekend with my family, I’ve realized that this city I once overlooked might just be the one other place I’d want to live if I ever left Madrid.

(Yoly, on the other hand, isn’t so sure. More on that below.)

So, here are three reasons I’d love to move to Bilbao—and two reasons I’d have second thoughts.

3 Reasons To Move to Bilbao

The weather

I love weather.

And by weather, I mean the full mix: wind, rain, and sun.

Walking around Bilbao reminded me of New Zealand, as if the air itself is alive with energy.

Yes, it rained while we were there, but the sun also came out, and I even got a bit sunburned! (I usually wear sunscreen, but I thought, “Hey, I’m in Bilbao—how bad could it be?”)

There’s an energy to the place that feels refreshing.

Sometimes I get tired of Madrid’s “desert” climate.

Don’t get me wrong—I love those warm nights sitting on a terrace in a t-shirt.

But summer is hot here, and Madrid doesn’t have a strong sense of four seasons.

The social scene

I’m not the bar-hopper I once was, but wow, the Basques know how to socialize.

The pintxos bars and restaurants were constantly packed while we were there.

And while this is true all over Spain, there’s a unique sense of community around pintxos (the Basque version of tapas) in Bilbao.

Young and old gather in the same places, and groups spill out onto terraces.

One thing I found fascinating is that in the Basque Country, you can very liberally take your drink and pintxo outside—even sit across the road on a bench with them—and no one minds.

I loved that sense of social trust.

Oh, and the pintxos are delicious (which you’ll see in the video coming this weekend).

The urban landscape

Walking around Bilbao, it simply feels very livable.

In recent years, the city has invested heavily in urban planning to make it a place you can truly enjoy.

The old town is vibrant, filled with locals out and about, and the walkways along the estuary are beautiful.

And, importantly for parents, the playgrounds are excellent.

​

2 Reasons NOT To Move to Bilbao

The weather

Yep, weather is on both lists.

If we were ever serious about moving to Bilbao, we’d definitely want to spend some time there in winter first.

Visiting in summer or autumn, when the days are still warm, is one thing—but living through December to February is another.

While Lucia was playing in a playground, we met a mother from Logroño who recently moved here.

She mentioned that the rain has been a challenge. So it’s something I’d need to explore further.

This for Yoly would be a deal breaker. She hates the wind, and said she’d likely tire of the rain.

(And if you’re thinking of moving anywhere, consider planning a scouting trip outside of the “ideal” months, so you can stress-test your preferred locations).

The location

One thing I love about Madrid is its central location.

It’s wonderfully connected by fast trains across Spain (and hopefully to more European destinations soon), and the airport offers flights everywhere.

As someone who travels a lot around Spain for work, living in Bilbao could make my job—especially making videos around Spain—a bit more challenging.

Madrid makes it easy to take quick trips all over the country (and Europe).

But if you haven't explored Bilbao, or have only check out its more famous sibling San Sebastian, I highly recommend adding this city to your itinerary!
