Is Marbella worth visiting?

So, let's talk Marbella.

Or Marbs, as Hanni likes to call it.

I went to Marbella to shoot a video with Andalusia expert Hanni Martini.

You'll remember Hanni from my other Malaga videos (here and here).

I'd always figured Marbella was just a tacky billionaire's paradise. All style over substance.

And yes, it is full of flashy cars and glamorous jetsetter types.

But here are a few things I learned that I didn't expect:

  • Marbella's old town is beautiful. Think gorgeous whitewashed buildings, narrow lanes, and beautiful shaded squares. When I imagined Marbella, I imagined superyachts and Gucci stores (and yes, there are those too). But I didn't expect this lovely Andalusian historic town.
  • You need to think of Marbella as more than just the town. The whole area of Marbella is much larger than just the historic town. The municipal area spreads up the coast, about 4-5 kilometers in each direction. Think beaches, beach restaurants (aka chiringuitos), and fancy hotels and homes right on the waterfront. Plus, a lovely seaside path in both directions.
  • Marbella has a 'Golden Mile'. If Marbella is glitzy, the city's so-called 'Golden Mile' is peak glitz. This area stretches west from the old town along the coast up to Puerto BanÃºs. I jogged along the water in this area and saw some very fancy homes and the classy, historic Marbella Club Hotel.â€‹
  • Marbella also has a 'Golden Triangle.' I've seen people talk online about the area from Marbella to Estepona on the coast and inland to BenahavÃ­s. These three towns create a triangle encompassing the most prime real estate on the Costa del Sol.
  • There is A LOT of substance here. We visited five stops on our shoot. Everything from Michelin-starred Japanese food to lightly grilled wagyu beef and some of the best cocktails I've ever drunk. And yes, a few more down-to-earth dishes. But across the board, the food was excellent. It was pricey, but not crazy pricey. In short, if you're looking to splurge, there are plenty of great dining options in the area.

So, is Marbella worth a spot on your Spain itinerary?

This might not be your destination if you want that rustic, old-school Spain experience (although the old town has some of that).

But if you want to add a luxury experience to your trip (perhaps a couple of nights at the famous Puente Romano Resort or Marbella Club Hotel or a "beach club day" at La Cabane,) I think it's definitely worth it.
