[Spain Update] This Spanish citizenship law ends in 2025 (but you can’t wait that long)

If you’ve got a Spanish grandparent, there’s a law available right now that could give you Spanish citizenship.

It’s called the Ley de Nietos (the “Grandchildren’s Law”)—officially known as the Ley de Memoria Democrática.

Broadly speaking, it was created to provide a path to citizenship for children and grandchildren of Spaniards who fled the country during the Civil War and dictatorship.

And it’s already working.

Over 100,000 people have been approved under the law.

But here’s the crucial part…

The law expires in October 2025.

(And there are no plans to extend it.)

Many law firms are planning to stop accepting new applications well before then—some by mid-year.

Why? Because these cases take time. You need to gather documents, submit your file, and wait for the Spanish consulate to process it.

So if this has been on your radar—or even just a vague “maybe one day” idea—now’s the time to act.

The good news? You don’t need to set foot in Spain to apply.

The entire process can be done from abroad, through your local Spanish consulate.

(The article happens to focus on applicants born in Mexico, but the law is not limited to Mexicans—it applies to eligible descendants around the world.)

P.S. I often get asked: What if it was my great-grandparent who was Spanish? In that case, the Ley de Nietos doesn’t apply directly. But—if your parent is still alive and qualifies through their Spanish grandparent, they may be able to apply first. If they’re granted citizenship, that could open a path for you too. So it’s still worth looking into.
