Spain Cancels Golden Visa – What’s Next?

After weeks of speculation, it’s official: last week, the Spanish Congress passed the law to cancel the Golden Visa program.

With the changes now underway, I turned to immigration consultant Marina Henestrosa of the firm Bureaucracy to answer the most common questions about the Golden Visa—and explore what options are still available for those dreaming of a move to Spain.

Here are my questions and her answers.

1. What is the current situation regarding the Golden Visa

The law officially canceling the Golden Visa was approved by Congress just last week.

However, it will take some time before it becomes effective.

As of today, the Golden Visa is still an option, and people can apply for it.

2. What are the next steps?

The next steps involve the Senate reviewing and approving the law.

Once the Senate approves it, it will return to Congress for final approval and then be published in the BOE (Boletin Oficial del Estado – where new laws are published), at which point it becomes effective.

3. When will it come into force?

Unfortunately, we don’t know the exact timeline yet.

Given the holiday season, we expect it could take up to two months before it becomes effective.

However, we expect that the Spanish Government will provide a “disposción transitoria” which is a rule that will allow for a transition period.

This will hopefully mean that those who are currently in the process of applying for a visa will not be affected when the law passes.

We will have a transition and an effective date soon.

4. What will happen to those currently on the Golden Visa? Will they be able to renew?

We currently don’t know this.

But soon, once the law's final text is approved, we will know how current visa holders could be impacted.

5. Do you recommend people still apply for the Golden Visa?

I am hesitant to recommend pursuing the Golden Visa right now.

The process involves several steps, including finding and purchasing the right property, which can take weeks or months.

Additionally, gathering documents like criminal records with an apostille and marriage certificates also requires time.

I am quite conservative, and I am not encouraging people to start the process right now.

6. What other key visa options are available?

The Non-Lucrative Visa and Digital Nomad Visa are excellent alternatives.

These visas allow clients to move to Spain and keep their plans intact.

While each case should be evaluated individually, the Non-Lucrative Visa is often the simplest route to moving to Spain, giving clients the opportunity to settle in, explore, and later purchase their dream home.

It currently requires you to show that you have €36,000 in savings (if applying as a couple) or €3,000 euros per month in passive income.
