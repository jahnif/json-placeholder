My 10 Tips for Learning Spanish As an Adult

Spanish has been my sparring partner for the 12 years I've lived in Spain.

Sometimes, I feel like I've got the better of it, and oftentimes, I feel like it has me on the ropes.

Plus, I've seen participants in my Move to Spain Masterclass grapple with the realities of having to learn the language.

Thankfully, I've spent the last year working with my excellent Spanish tutor, Alicia, and that's provided me with tremendous support and learning.

Here are my ten tips, especially useful for those moving to Spain, but with takeaways for everyone.

(I gave my brother a version of these, given he's not looking at living in Spain).

  1. You will not learn Spanish through osmosis. i.e., You will not "pick it up" in Spain.
  2. Strong language skills will make your transition to life in Spain smoother and your experience in Spain richer â€“ that's a guarantee.
  3. Learning a language as an adult requires regular, structured effort. It's more than a casual coffee-time activity. You need a solid plan.
  4. Aim for functionality over fluency. Fluency is elusive, but being functional? Thatâ€™s about having the Spanish level necessary to live your ideal life in Spain, which varies for each person.
  5. Embrace the language learning rollercoaster. Some months, you'll soar with confidence; others, you'll stumble. It's trite to say, but it's true: there is no final destination; it's simply an ongoing journey.
  6. Regardless of how much you learn before moving to Spain, it will be hard when you first get here. You will feel exhausted after a dinner with Spanish speakers. Your brain hasn't had to work so hard in years. Feel the burn (and feel content knowing that learning a second language is like bicep curls for the brain).
  7. If you're Spain-bound, find a tutor familiar with Spain's Spanish, ideally someone from your future city. Language and culture are intertwined, and your tutor is your guide to both.
  8. Duolingo and other apps are great, but they're just part of your toolkit, alongside books, music, and Spanish Netflix with subtitles. None of these tools are effective in isolation. See the next tip.
  9. You need a study plan, i.e., a language learning roadmap based on your level and objectives. Get one from a tutor or through a course tailored to your needs. Your needs are defined as your level plus your reason for learning Spanish (to talk to your niece or to live and thrive in Spain?) Once you have a study plan, all the tools (Duolingo, books, music) will act as pillars to support that plan.
  10. Start learning now. Trust me. Don't wait. (This is the most important tip).
