I sat in the interview and couldn’t speak.

Words literally would not come out of my mouth, try as I might.

The interviewer looked at me like I was crazy.

I wished the floor would swallow me whole.

This was around two years after I moved to Spain.

The first 12 months had been incredible.

I was obsessed with everything.

Every small interaction, every trip to the market, every café con leche felt like stepping into a new world.

I was devouring books about Spain, exploring new places, soaking up every detail.

My Spanish wasn’t great, but it didn’t matter. I was new, and people gave me a pass. I smiled, I tried, and that was enough.

But then? Things got HARD.

About 12 months in, I quit my job directing TV commercials.

I’d come to Spain with that job lined up, but now, I wanted something different.

I wanted to write about Spain—to tell its stories, to land a byline in the New York Times travel section.

But after six months of writing articles that nobody bought (or even replied to my emails about)…

Money was running low.

Yoly and I were stressed.

My Spanish wasn’t improving as fast as I had hoped.

I wasn’t sleeping well.

I developed a weird throat issue where, in tense situations, I literally couldn’t speak.

That’s how I ended up in an interview for an English-language teaching magazine—desperate for something to keep us afloat.

And yet, there I was, unable to get words out.

For the first time, I wondered what the hell I was doing. Was this a mistake? Where were we going with this?

But looking back, I was at a key moment in my Spain journey.

A lot of people think moving to Spain (or anywhere) is like flipping a switch:

✅ Before Spain = Planning.

✅ After Spain = Dream life unlocked.

But the truth?

Moving to Spain is just the beginning.

See below...

Your graph may look different.

As the saying goes, "actual results may vary."

But in short, it ain't gonna be a straight line up.

And if you don’t expect these bumps (as I didn’t), you start to doubt everything.

You wonder if you made the wrong decision.

And—no promises—but you might even develop some strange physical symptoms. (Hopefully not!)

The key is going in eyes wide open.

That’s why, when I talk to Move to Spain Masterclass participants, I make sure to bring up this idea: your Spain journey will evolve in phases.

Yes, the move itself is important, but what comes after is what really shapes your life here.

And yet, most of us spend 99% of our energy figuring out how to move to Spain and almost none thinking about what happens next.

Which, for many, is the rest of our lives.

I didn’t have the graph back then. But I did find a way to climb out of the hole.

I started experimenting and making adjustments.

  • I made a real effort to improve my Spanish so I could integrate better.
  • I started guiding food tours (which led to me co-founding Devour Tours).
  • I built my own social network instead of relying on my wife's.

And slowly by surely, the curve evened out.

I don’t have the extreme honeymoon highs anymore (though sitting in an old-man bar, chowing down on a home-cooked menú del día still thrills me).

But I also don’t have the lows.

In short, I’ve built a life.

In Spain.

Which was the point in the first place, right?
