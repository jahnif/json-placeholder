My 5 Top Places to Eat in Malaga

I've featured almost 20 tapas bars and restaurants in my last two Malaga videos.

So if I was going to take you to just five over a long weekend, which would they be?

(To make the list more vivid, I've linked to the moment we visit each place in my videos).

Here goes!

1. Mercado Atarazanas

This isn't your typical restaurant - it's a bustling market. One of Spain's most lively, colorful, and fun to explore. Inside, you'll find superb tapas bars serving market-fresh food. Imagine savoring grilled seafood and raw clams amidst the lively market atmosphere. Don't miss the nearby Antigua Casa de Guardia, an old tavern founded in 1840, where you can enjoy famous Malaga wine straight from the barrels.

​Watch the video​

​Location on Google Maps​

2. Mesón Mariano

My absolute favorite restaurant/tapas bar in Malaga. Mariano and family offer Andalusian classics in a rustic, cozy ambiance. They have a penchant for artichokes, preparing them in various ways when in season. Ensure you book ahead to guarantee a table. Alternatively, arrive early to snag a spot in the bar area.

​Watch the video​

​Location on Google Maps​

3. El Caleño

The classic Malaga experience is eating at a chiringuito (seaside restaurant) in the old fishing barrio Pedregalejo. And El Caleño is considered one of the best. It hits all the right notes: fresh fish and seafood cooked over an open flame on the beach, tables in the sun (and inside), and efficient, no-fuss Spanish service. A long, sun-drenched lunch here epitomizes the Malaga experience. Remember, weekend reservations are a must.

​Watch the video​

​Location on Google Maps​

4. El Tintero

Similar to El Caleño, it offers fresh grilled seafood and typical dishes. But El Tintero – another Malaga classic open for decades – has a key point of difference. The ordering system. Here, the waiters weave through the packed restaurant with plates of freshly cooked seafood, and you flag them down for your choice. It's like dim sum Spanish style. We arrived a little late for this vibe in the video, but at lunchtime on a weekend, the buzzing ambiance and fresh-off-the-grill seafood are a perfect pair.

​Watch the video​

​Location on Google Maps​

5. Misuto

This restaurant blew me away. Here, the chefs harness Malaga's fantastic local seafood to make sushi and other Asian-inspired dishes. The nigiri burst with flavor, and a standout was the roasted bone marrow mixed with shrimp tartar and topped with fresh truffle. I'd consider jumping on the fast train from Madrid just to have lunch here again (and this time take Yoly!).
